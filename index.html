Same Journey, Deeper Lens

Each action above, broken down: what she does, what she touches, what she needs, where it hurts, and how AI could help — action by action, not a separate theory.

7:00 AMDock Check
Behavior
Walks straight to the dock, physically counts crates against a paper indent copy, cross-references from memory what she ordered yesterday.
Touchpoints
Paper delivery note, physical crates, vendor delivery person.
Requirements
Delivery quantities need to match the order; shortfalls need to be caught immediately, not mid-day.
Pain Points
No system cross-check — pure manual count vs. paper. Discrepancies are often found too late to action same-day.
HMW (AI)
Surface the expected indent next to a tap-to-confirm received-quantity flow, flagging shortfalls instantly instead of relying on memory of what was ordered.
7:30 AMFresh Produce Quality Inspection
Behavior
Visually/physically inspects crates, judges ripeness by touch and smell, rejects substandard units on the spot.
Touchpoints
The produce itself, the vendor's delivery person — no recording tool of any kind.
Requirements
A fast, consistent way to judge quality; vendor accountability over time.
Pain Points
Entirely subjective, entirely undocumented. The same vendor can send bad produce repeatedly with no pattern ever surfacing.
HMW (AI)
Let her snap one photo per rejected crate so vendor quality issues accumulate into a trackable score instead of disappearing into memory.
8:00 AMStaff Briefing & Positioning
Behavior
Verbally briefs whoever showed up, assigns floor positions, improvises on the spot if someone's absent.
Touchpoints
Staff (verbal only), WhatsApp group (used after the fact, never predictively).
Requirements
Needs to know about absences before arriving, not at the door.
Pain Points
Last-minute absences force a full mental re-plan under time pressure, right as the store opens.
HMW (AI)
Surface staff availability and a suggested floor plan the night before, so the morning starts with a plan instead of a scramble.
9:00 AMMorning Peak Rush
Behavior
Monitors queue length by eye/ear, reactively pulls a floor staffer to billing — leaving produce unsupervised.
Touchpoints
Billing counters (visual), the queue itself, staff (verbal redirect).
Requirements
Needs to anticipate the rush, not just react; needs to redeploy without creating a second gap.
Pain Points
Purely reactive — by the time she notices, customers are already waiting. Pulling staff from produce trades one gap for another.
HMW (AI)
Predict the rush 20–30 minutes out from historical POS patterns, so she repositions staff before the queue forms.
11:00 AMStockout Chase
Behavior
Hears about an empty shelf from a customer or staff, checks the stockroom herself, calls the DC if that's also empty.
Touchpoints
Phone call to DC, physical stockroom check, verbal report.
Requirements
Needs to know about gaps before a customer does; needs a faster resolution path than a cold call.
Pain Points
Always discovered after the fact. Resolution depends entirely on who picks up at the DC — no guaranteed response time.
HMW (AI)
Alert her to stockout risk while there's still backup stock in the stockroom or time to request a top-up — before the shelf actually empties.
12:00 PMFresh Produce Order Decision🔴 Highest pain
Behavior
Recalls yesterday's sales from memory/printout, estimates stockroom inventory by eye, decides quantities by gut feel, types into the Oracle ERP indent form.
Touchpoints
POS printout, physical stockroom walk, Oracle ERP indent screen, an old Excel file.
Requirements
A reliable demand signal per SKU; an ordering interface that takes minutes, not an hour.
Pain Points
45–60 minutes of pure guesswork — the highest-stakes decision of her day gets the least support.
HMW (AI)
Generate a daily order recommendation from 30-day sales history that she just reviews and approves. → Screen 1 / UC-01
2:00 PMAfternoon Floor Walk
Behavior
Walks the floor hunting for near-expiry items and shelf problems using only her eyes and a paper notepad.
Touchpoints
Paper notepad, visual inspection — no system access during the walk itself.
Requirements
Needs to know what's near-expiry before the walk, not discover it by chance during it.
Pain Points
Entirely reactive discovery. Anything she misses on this one walk may not get marked down in time at all.
HMW (AI)
Hand her a pre-generated near-expiry list before she starts, turning the walk into verification instead of discovery.
4:00 PMMarkdown Decision
Behavior
Decides markdown % per item by instinct, manually re-labels with a price gun.
Touchpoints
Price gun, the product itself, her own experience.
Requirements
Needs to know what markdown rate actually clears stock vs. leaves money on the table.
Pain Points
No historical reference. Marks down too little (stays unsold) or too much (loses margin needlessly) — pure guesswork either way.
HMW (AI)
Recommend a markdown % per item based on hours-to-expiry and historical sell-through, so this isn't a guess.
5:00 PMEvening Peak Rush
Behavior
Same reactive pattern as 9am — queue-watching, staff reshuffling — but now she's been on her feet for 10 hours.
Touchpoints
Same as morning rush.
Requirements
Same predictive need as the morning, compounded by fatigue.
Pain Points
The same reactive pattern repeats, except her judgment is measurably worse by 5pm than it was at 9am.
HMW (AI)
Apply the same predictive staffing alert here too, so she isn't relying on depleted end-of-day judgment for the second rush.
7:00 PMCash Reconciliation
Behavior
Physically counts each till, manually matches totals against the POS report, logs discrepancies on paper.
Touchpoints
Physical cash, calculator, paper log, POS report.
Requirements
Discrepancies need to be flagged automatically, not found by manually re-checking everything.
Pain Points
Time-consuming and compliance-critical, fully manual. One small math error creates a discrepancy that takes even longer to chase down.
HMW (AI)
Auto-match POS totals against till counts and surface only the discrepancies, instead of making her verify everything that already matches.
8:00 PMDaily HQ Report🔴 Highest pain
Behavior
Pulls numbers from a POS printout, stockroom memory, and a wastage tally; manually compiles into Excel; types a WhatsApp summary.
Touchpoints
POS printout, Excel, WhatsApp, memory.
Requirements
The report should write itself from data that already exists in the systems.
Pain Points
45–60 minutes at the most exhausted point of her day. Error-prone, and inconsistent in format vs. other stores' reports.
HMW (AI)
Auto-draft the report directly from POS + inventory data, so she's reviewing and sending instead of compiling from scratch. → Screen 2 / UC-02
9:00 PMClose & Leave
Behavior
Locks up, mentally runs through "did I miss anything," goes home.
Touchpoints
Physical lock, her own mental checklist.
Requirements
Confidence nothing was missed, without re-checking everything herself.
Pain Points
Lingering anxiety — was tomorrow's order logged right? Did that markdown register correctly?
HMW (AI)
Give her a single end-of-day confirmation — "everything's logged, tomorrow's order is ready to review" — so she leaves with closure instead of lingering doubt.

What the personas tell us about Roz
01
Design for Radhika, not for Vikram
Radhika is the primary user. Every interaction must work on a mid-range Android, take under 2 minutes, and feel like WhatsApp. Vikram benefits passively through better data — he doesn't need a separate interface in V1.
02
Trust is earned through accuracy, not features
Radhika has 8 years of gut-feel expertise. If Roz's first order recommendation is wrong, she'll never use it again. The AI must explain its reasoning ("Based on last Tuesday's sales + tomorrow's local holiday") so she can verify it matches her instinct.
03
She must stay in control
Radhika doesn't want an AI that orders for her — she wants one that does the research and hands her a recommendation to approve. The approve/adjust model is critical. "AI suggests, I decide" is the mental model that will get adoption.
04
Hindi first, features second
Radhika thinks in Marathi, switches to Hindi, uses English numbers. The interface must lead in Hindi (or her regional language) with English numbers/units. Voice input is a natural extension — she's already using voice messages on WhatsApp.
